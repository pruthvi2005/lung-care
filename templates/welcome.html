Common Risk Factors

Understanding these factors can help in early detection and prevention.

Smoking

Cigarette smoking is the number one risk factor for lung cancer, responsible for about 80% of lung cancer deaths.

Radon Exposure

Radon is the second leading cause of lung cancer in the United States, responsible for about 21,000 lung cancer deaths each year.

Air Pollution

Long-term exposure to air pollution can increase the risk of developing lung cancer, especially in urban areas.
